--- Task 390: Cut trees or logs.
[A]
  1. [IC] Review the trail map and identify the path to be cleared.
  2. [NA] Put on personal protective equipment (PPE), including hard hat, safety glasses, ear protection, and steel-toed boots.
  3. [PD] Inspect the felling area for any hazards, such as power lines, rock outcrops, or animal nests.
  4. [PD] Select the first tree to be felled based on its species, size, and proximity to the path.
  5. [NA] Start the chainsaw and ensure it is functioning properly.
  6. [ME] Make the undercut (the first cut) on the side of the tree where it is intended to fall.
  7. [ME] Make the back cut (the second cut) on the opposite side of the tree, slightly above the undercut.
  8. [PD] Monitor the tree's movement and guide it to fall in the intended direction.
  9. [FV] Allow the tree to fall completely and ensure it lands safely without causing damage.
  10. [FV] Turn off the chainsaw and secure it.
  11. [FV] Inspect the cleared path for any remaining hazards or debris and remove them if necessary.
[B]
  1. [PD] Inspect the marked tree for lean, surrounding obstacles, and potential hazards like dead branches.
  2. [ME] Clear a safe working area around the base of the tree, removing brush and loose debris.
  3. [NA] Position the chainsaw at the designated felling notch, ensuring proper stance and grip.
  4. [ME] Engage the chainsaw and make the initial cut, following the planned notch angle.
  5. [ME] Continue cutting through the tree trunk, applying consistent pressure and monitoring the felling direction.
  6. [PD] Listen for the cracking sound indicating the tree is about to fall.
  7. [NA] Move to a safe distance in the planned escape route as the tree begins to fall.
  8. [FV] Visually confirm that the tree has fallen in the intended direction and clear of obstacles.
  9. [ME] Buck the felled tree into manageable sections using the chainsaw.
  10. [FV] Inspect the cleared path for any remaining hazards or debris and remove them if necessary.
  11. [IC] Communicate with the trail crew to confirm the path has been successfully cleared and is ready for marking.
[C]
  1. [PD] Assess the tree's condition, surrounding environment, and potential hazards like dead branches or power lines.
  2. [NA] Don safety equipment including helmet, gloves, eye protection, and appropriate footwear.
  3. [ME] Clear a safe working area around the tree base, removing brush and loose debris.
  4. [PD] Plan the felling direction, considering the tree's lean and surrounding obstacles.
  5. [ME] Make the undercut and back cut, following proper chainsaw techniques to avoid kickback.
  6. [PD] Monitor the tree's movement and guide it to fall in the intended direction, moving to a safe distance as it begins to fall.
  7. [FV] Confirm the tree has fallen safely and clear of obstacles.
  8. [FV] Turn off the chainsaw and secure it.
[D]
  1. [PD] Review the work area and identify the tree(s) to be cut, considering their condition, surrounding environment, and intended falling direction.
  2. [NA] Put on safety equipment, including helmet, gloves, protective eyewear, and other necessary PPE.
  3. [NA] Start the chainsaw and ensure it is functioning properly.
  4. [ME] Make the undercut (first cut) on the side of the tree where it is intended to fall, at a 60-70° angle.
  5. [ME] Make the back cut (second cut) on the opposite side of the tree, slightly above the undercut, until the tree begins to lean.
  6. [PD] Monitor the tree's movement, guide it to fall in the intended direction, and ensure it lands safely without causing damage.
  7. [FV] Allow the tree to fall completely, then turn off the chainsaw and secure it.
  8. [FV] Remove safety equipment and inspect it for any damage.
  9. [IC] Document the work completed, report any issues or observations, and communicate with the team regarding the next steps.
  10. [FV] Inspect the work area for any remaining hazards or debris and remove them if necessary.
