--- Task 272: Inspect industrial or commercial equipment to ensure proper operation.
[A]
  1. [IC] Review the equipment's operational manual and recent maintenance logs to identify the next item for inspection.
  2. [NA] Navigate to the equipment, ensuring the area is clear of obstacles and hazards.
  3. [ME] Don personal protective equipment (PPE) such as safety glasses and gloves.
  4. [PD] Visually inspect the equipment's exterior for obvious damage, leaks, or wear, using a flashlight for hard-to-see areas.
  5. [PD] Listen to the equipment's operation for any unusual noises or vibrations.
  6. [PD] Feel the exterior of the equipment for any unusual heat or cold spots using the back of the hand.
  7. [PD] Inspect critical components such as belts, pulleys, filters, and safety guards for signs of wear, damage, or improper operation.
  8. [PD] Measure and record key operational parameters such as voltage, current, temperature, and airflow using appropriate sensors.
  9. [FV] Document findings, including any issues or areas for improvement, in a digital maintenance log.
  10. [FV] Report any issues or required maintenance to the supervisor or maintenance team.
[B]
  1. [IC] Review the equipment's operational manual and any recent maintenance logs.
  2. [NA] Approach the air compressor and ensure the area is clear of obstacles and hazards.
  3. [PD] Check the air compressor's gauges and readouts to ensure they are within the specified operating parameters.
  4. [PD] Inspect the air compressor's belts and pulleys for signs of wear or damage.
  5. [PD] Verify that all safety guards and protective covers are in place and secure.
  6. [FV] Record any findings and observations in the maintenance log.
  7. [FV] Report any issues or required maintenance to the supervisor or maintenance team.
  8. [ME] Perform any necessary repairs or maintenance on the conveyor belt system, using specialized tools and equipment.
  9. [FV] Verify that the conveyor belt system is operating within normal parameters and document the results.
  10. [FV] Update the maintenance schedule and production logs to reflect the inspection results and any necessary corrective actions.
[C]
  1. [IC] Review equipment documentation (manuals, logs) and identify specific inspection points.
  2. [NA] Navigate to the equipment, ensuring a clear and safe path.
  3. [ME] Don appropriate Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) as specified for the equipment.
  4. [PD] Conduct a visual inspection, checking for obvious damage, leaks, wear, or unusual conditions.
  5. [PD] Use specialized tools (gauges, sensors, etc.) to measure operational parameters and compare to specifications.
  6. [PD] Listen for unusual noises during operation and feel for excessive vibrations or heat.
  7. [FV] Document all findings, including observations, measurements, and any potential issues.
  8. [FV] Report any significant issues or required maintenance to the appropriate personnel.
[D]
  1. [IC] Review the equipment's operational manual, maintenance logs, and digital floor plans to familiarize with the inspection route, critical components, and operating parameters.
  2. [NA] Don personal protective equipment (PPE) and navigate to the equipment, using facility maps and navigation systems if necessary.
  3. [NA] Ensure the area is clear of obstacles and hazards, and approach the equipment.
  4. [PD] Visually inspect the equipment's exterior, checking for obvious damage, leaks, corrosion, or loose connections.
  5. [PD] Use appropriate tools (e.g., flashlight, borescope, mirror) to inspect hard-to-see areas and document findings with notes or photos.
  6. [PD] Measure relevant parameters (e.g., temperature, pressure, voltage, current, airflow) using appropriate sensors, comparing readings to established baselines.
  7. [PD] Listen for unusual noises and feel the equipment for excessive heat or cold spots.
  8. [PD] Inspect safety guards, protective covers, and other safety features to ensure they are in place and secure.
  9. [ME] Perform any necessary maintenance tasks, such as cleaning, lubricating, tightening, or replacing components.
  10. [FV] Record observations, findings, and performed maintenance tasks in a digital maintenance log, and update relevant schedules and logs.
  11. [FV] Review inspection findings with relevant teams, implement corrective actions, and verify that the equipment is operating within normal parameters.
