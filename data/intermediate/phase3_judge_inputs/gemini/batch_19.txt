Criteria:
  1. Completeness  -- no critical steps missing
  2. Realism       -- every step is concretely executable
  3. Stage accuracy -- stage label correct for each step:
     IC=Intent_Communication  NA=Navigation_Addressing
     PD=Perception_Diagnosis  ME=Manipulation_Execution  FV=Feedback_Verification
  4. Conciseness   -- no redundant or duplicate steps
  5. Scope         -- starts and ends at exactly the right point

OUTPUT: fill the ANSWER TEMPLATE at the bottom.
Format: N: X | one-sentence reason (max 12 words)
IMPORTANT: Each task has a different best answer. Do NOT pick the same
letter for every task without reading.

--- Task 148: Level machines or equipment.
[A]
  1. [IC] Consult the machine's manual to understand its leveling process and safety precautions.
  2. [NA] Locate the leveling screws or jacks on the machine's base, usually at each corner.
  3. [PD] Use a precision level gauge to measure the machine's current alignment.
  4. [ME] Adjust the leveling screws or jacks in small increments to bring the machine closer to level.
  5. [FV] Re-measure the machine's alignment using the precision level gauge to ensure it is within the specified tolerance.
  6. [ME] Insert thin shims under the feet if necessary to fine-tune the leveling.
  7. [FV] Double-check the level readings to confirm the machine is properly leveled.
  8. [FV] Document the leveling process, the adjustments made, and the final alignment measurements for future reference.
[B]
  1. [NA] Retrieve the precision level gauge and other necessary tools from the tool cabinet.
  2. [PD] Position the precision level gauge on the printing press to measure its current level.
  3. [PD] Read the initial level measurement from the gauge and record the deviation from level.
  4. [PD] Identify the specific areas of the press that need adjustment based on the gauge readings.
  5. [ME] Remove the leveling shims from the press where adjustments are needed.
  6. [ME] Insert new shims or adjust the existing ones to bring the press closer to level.
  7. [PD] Reposition the precision level gauge to measure the new level of the press.
  8. [PD] Read the new level measurement and record the deviation from level.
  9. [ME] Repeat steps 5-8 until the press is perfectly level.
  10. [FV] Secure the leveling shims and ensure the press is stable and level.
[C]
  1. [NA] Don appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE) and safety gear.
  2. [NA] Locate the leveling screws or jacks on the machine's base.
  3. [PD] Use a spirit level or digital level to measure the machine's current alignment.
  4. [ME] Adjust the leveling screws or jacks to bring the machine closer to level.
  5. [FV] Re-measure the machine's alignment and compare it to the desired level.
  6. [ME] Repeat steps 4 and 5 until the machine is level within the specified tolerance.
  7. [FV] Double-check the machine's stability by gently rocking it.
[D]

  2. [NA] Don appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE) and safety gear.
  3. [NA] Locate the leveling points (screws, jacks, or feet) on the machine's base.
  4. [PD] Use a spirit level or digital level to measure the machine's current alignment in both longitudinal and transverse directions.
  5. [PD] Calculate the necessary adjustments based on the measured alignment and the manufacturer's specifications.
  6. [ME] Using a wrench, socket, or other appropriate tool, make the necessary adjustments to the leveling points in small increments.
  7. [FV] Periodically re-measure the machine's alignment using the spirit level or digital level to verify progress.
