EXPERT REVIEW -- Micro-Action Sequence Evaluation
For each task, read ALL four options, then choose the BEST (A/B/C/D).

Criteria:
  1. Completeness  -- no critical steps missing
  2. Realism       -- every step is concretely executable
  3. Stage accuracy -- stage label correct for each step:
     IC=Intent_Communication  NA=Navigation_Addressing
     PD=Perception_Diagnosis  ME=Manipulation_Execution  FV=Feedback_Verification
  4. Conciseness   -- no redundant or duplicate steps
  5. Scope         -- starts and ends at exactly the right point

OUTPUT: fill the ANSWER TEMPLATE at the bottom.
Format: N: X | one-sentence reason (max 12 words)
IMPORTANT: Each task has a different best answer. Do NOT pick the same
letter for every task without reading.

--- Task 344: Cut tile, stone, or other masonry materials.

  2. [NA] Select the appropriate cutting tool, such as a wet saw or a diamond blade saw, based on the tile's material.

  4. [ME] Operate the cutting tool to make the cut, applying steady pressure and maintaining a consistent speed.
  5. [FV] Inspect the cut edge for smoothness and accuracy, and use a tile file or grinder to smooth any rough edges if necessary.
  6. [FV] Dry-fit the cut tile in the intended area to ensure it fits correctly and make any necessary adjustments.
[B]

  4. [NA] Position the tile on the cutting mat of the tile cutter.
  5. [NA] Align the cutting wheel of the tile cutter with the marked circle.
  6. [PD] Apply pressure and move the tile cutter along the marked circle to score the tile.
  7. [ME] Snap the tile along the scored line to complete the cut.
  8. [FV] Inspect the cut edge for any rough spots or imperfections.
  9. [ME] Use a tile file to smooth any rough edges or imperfections.
  10. [FV] Place the cut tile around the plumbing fixture to ensure it fits perfectly.
[C]

  2. [NA] Select the appropriate cutting tool (wet saw, tile nippers, etc.) based on the tile material and cut complexity.

  4. [ME] Execute the cut using the selected tool, applying steady pressure and controlled movement.

[D]

  4. [ME] Secure the tile in the cutting tool, ensuring it is stable and the cutting line is aligned with the blade.
  5. [ME] Operate the cutting tool to make the first cut, applying steady pressure and maintaining a consistent speed.
  6. [FV] Inspect the cut and use tile nippers, a grinder, or a tile file to make any necessary adjustments or smooth rough edges.
  7. [ME] Repeat steps 3-6 for all necessary tiles, ensuring each tile fits perfectly within the required area.
  8. [FV] Dry-fit the cut tiles in the area to ensure they fit correctly and make any necessary adjustments to the cuts.
  9. [ME] Once satisfied with the fit, remove the tiles and prepare them for installation by applying thinset mortar to the back.
  10. [ME] Install the cut and prepared tiles in the area, ensuring they are level, properly spaced, and aligned with one another.
