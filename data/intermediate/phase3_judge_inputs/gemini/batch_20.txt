--- Task 157: Administer cancer treatments.
[A]
  1. [IC] Review the patient's electronic health record (EHR) to understand their medical history, current treatment plan, and any allergies or contraindications.
  2. [NA] Check the patient's identification band to ensure it matches the EHR and confirm the patient's identity.
  3. [PD] Calculate the correct dosage of chemotherapy drugs based on the patient's weight, height, and kidney function.
  4. [NA] Gather the necessary chemotherapy drugs, syringes, IV tubing, and other supplies needed for the treatment.
  5. [ME] Prepare the chemotherapy drugs by drawing up the correct dosage into syringes and mixing them if necessary.
  6. [PD] Access the patient's IV line and verify that it is patent and functioning correctly.
  7. [ME] Administer the chemotherapy drugs slowly and evenly over the prescribed period, monitoring the patient's vital signs throughout the process.
  8. [ME] Flush the IV line with normal saline to ensure that all chemotherapy drugs have been administered and to prevent any residual drug from being administered later.
  9. [FV] Document the administration of the chemotherapy drugs in the patient's EHR, including the time of administration, dosage, and any observed side effects or reactions.
  10. [FV] Monitor the patient for any signs of adverse reactions to the chemotherapy drugs, such as nausea, vomiting, or allergic reactions.
  11. [IC] Once the patient is stable and there are no immediate adverse reactions, inform the patient's oncologist or the treatment center's medical team about the administration of the chemotherapy drugs.
[B]

  2. [ME] Prepare the chemotherapy agents and equipment according to the manufacturer's instructions and hospital protocols.
  3. [ME] Administer the chemotherapy agents to the patient through an IV infusion, monitoring the patient's vital signs and adjusting the infusion as needed.
  4. [PD] Monitor the patient's vital signs and adjust the infusion as needed to ensure the patient receives the correct dose.
  5. [IC] Communicate effectively with the patient and other healthcare professionals to ensure a smooth and safe administration of chemotherapy.
  6. [FV] Document the administration of chemotherapy in the patient's medical record, including any adjustments made to the treatment plan.
  7. [FV] Dispose of any hazardous materials and clean and sterilize the equipment according to hospital protocols.
  8. [PD] Review the patient's response to the treatment and adjust the treatment plan as needed for future administrations.
  9. [IC] Communicate the patient's response to the treatment to other healthcare professionals and document any changes to the treatment plan.
  10. [FV] Maintain accurate and up-to-date records of the patient's treatment, including any adjustments made to the treatment plan.
  11. [PD] Continuously monitor the patient's vital signs and adjust the infusion as needed to ensure the patient receives the correct dose.
  12. [ME] Administer any additional treatments or medications as needed, and document the administration in the patient's medical record.
[C]
  1. [IC] Verify patient identity and treatment plan against the doctor's orders.
  2. [NA] Gather necessary equipment: IV bag, chemotherapy drug vial, saline solution, gloves, IV tubing, alcohol swabs.
  3. [ME] Prepare the chemotherapy drug according to the precise dosage instructions, using aseptic technique.
  4. [ME] Clean the patient's IV insertion site with alcohol swabs and insert the IV needle.
  5. [ME] Connect the IV tubing to the chemotherapy drug bag and prime the line, ensuring no air bubbles.
  6. [ME] Administer the chemotherapy drug slowly through the IV line, continuously monitoring the patient's vital signs (blood pressure, heart rate, oxygen saturation).
  7. [FV] Document the treatment administration details, including dosage, time, and any observed side effects, in the patient's electronic medical record.
  8. [NA] Dispose of used equipment according to hospital safety protocols.

  1. [IC] Review patient's EHR for medical history, current treatment plan, allergies, and contraindications.
  2. [NA] Verify patient's identity by checking their ID band against EHR records.
  3. [PD] Calculate precise dosage of chemotherapy drugs based on patient's weight, height, and kidney function.
  4. [NA] Gather necessary equipment and chemotherapy drugs according to the treatment plan.
  5. [ME] Prepare chemotherapy drugs by drawing up the correct dosage into syringes and mixing them if necessary.
  6. [PD] Access the patient's IV line, verify its patency, and prime the line to remove air bubbles.
  7. [ME] Administer chemotherapy drugs slowly and evenly over the prescribed period, monitoring vital signs throughout.
  8. [ME] Flush the IV line with normal saline to ensure complete drug administration and prevent residual drug from being administered later.
  9. [FV] Document administration details, including time, dosage, and observed side effects, in the patient's EHR.
  10. [FV] Monitor the patient for signs of adverse reactions to chemotherapy drugs, such as nausea, vomiting, or allergic reactions.
  11. [IC] Inform the patient's oncologist or medical team about the administration of chemotherapy drugs once the patient is stable.
